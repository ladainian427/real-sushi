Sushi Info

Coming from the country of Japan, many new foods and foreign phrases in sushi culture may confuse those who have no prior exposure to the cuisine. Even those who do often find themselves lost in the magnitude of fish names and jargon. With the right resources, navigating the world of sushi will become a much smoother journey.

Rare and Unique Sushi

Though what we know today as "sushi" has been around for over a century, the cuisine is always changing. New rolls and creative fusion cuisine is created all the time. Aside from new things, there are also the types of sushi many Westerners never encounter or simply refuse to eat.

Top Pages for Unique Sushi

The following are sites containing information about unique and more uncommon types of sushi.

MyYearInJapan | Horsemeat

This blog post writes about an experience eating a relatively uncommon horsemeat sushi while in Japan. She posts a few pictures and describes the experience.

Shizuoka Sushi | General

A very good page with examples of unique and uncommon types of sushi and seafood that one may encounter. Lots of pictures and descriptions of these foods which the author describes as "acquired tastes."

Ten Hostel Osaka | Liver & Fish Milt

A short, but interesting blog post about a dining experience of liver and fish milt/sperm (shirako) sushi. Has interesting pictures, though the captions aren't very descriptive.

Is Sushi Healthy? | Fusion

This page goes over some suggestions on uncommon rolls and sushi to try. Also describes the concept of newly created sushi rolls. Like other fusion recipes, anything can happen!

Sushirrito | Fusion

The webpage of a fusion restaurant in San Francisco combining sushi with latin-infused ingredients and forms.
