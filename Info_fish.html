Fish

When it comes to ordering sushi rolls, trying to determine what the roll consists of based only on the name is not entirely practical. Although most restaurants create their own special rolls, there are the common ones that are known to almost everyone, such as the California Roll or the Spicy Tuna Roll. Below is a comprehensive list of sites that provide the names of most rolls and the ingredients that you will find in them.

Top Pages for Sushi Rolls

The following are sites that list and describe different types of sushi rolls that you can find in most restaurants. THe following lists will provide a detailed explanation of ingredients and what the rolls consist of.

Types of Sushi Fish & Seafood | Foodservice Warehouse

Foodservice Warehouse has the most extensive list of Fish and Seafood used in Sushi and Sashimi. They organize the information in multiple ways, They first have a table organizing the fish, roe, mollusks, and crustaceans used in Sushi. Then they elaborate on each type giving the Japanese name and a short description of it.

Sushi and Sashimi Glossary | The Nibble

The Nibble has a friendly and easy to navigate site with all the different fishes used in Sushi and Sashimi listed by their Japanese/Sushi roll name. Their glossary is alphabetically arranged and also has photographs to go along with it.

Popular Types of Sushi Fish | DIY Sushi Recipes

A fairly simple and straightforward site, DIY Sushi recipes lists the most popular fishes used in making sushi. They arrange the sushi by how it is served -- either raw or cooked.

Sushi Roll Recipe | Rolls

This is a very basic site focusing on the basic preparation and ingredients of the roll. In addition, there are also cook notes to show how the roll was made. This site is useful if you want to know how a particular roll was made.
