Sushi Info

Coming from the country of Japan, many new foods and foreign phrases in sushi culture may confuse those who have no prior exposure to the cuisine. Even those who do often find themselves lost in the magnitude of fish names and jargon. With the right resources, navigating the world of sushi will become a much smoother journey.

Fish Types

When trying to order to sushi, there are incredible amounts of different types of fish that can be ordered. The common ones like salmon and tuna are self explanatory but there are also many ones that most amateurs will not know, like toro.

Top Pages for Fish Types

The following are sites that list and describe the different types of sushi fish.

Food Service Warehouse | Fish Types

Foodservice Warehouse has the most extensive list of Fish and Seafood used in Sushi and Sashimi. They organize the information in multiple ways, They first have a table organizing the fish, roe, mollusks, and crustaceans used in Sushi. Then they elaborate on each type giving the Japanese name and a short description of it.

The Nibble | Fish Types

The Nibble has a friendly and easy to navigate site with all the different fishes used in Sushi and Sashimi listed by their Japanese/Sushi roll name. Their glossary is alphabetically arranged and also has photographs to go along with it.

DIY Sushi Recipes | Fish Types

A fairly simple and straightforward site, DIY Sushi recipes lists the most popular fishes used in making sushi. They arrange the sushi by how it is served -- either raw or cooked.
