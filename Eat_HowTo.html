Eat

Sushi, and Japanese cuisine in general, may seem daunting to many newcomers. Though there is no right or wrong way to eat sushi, there are guidelines that define what is considered proper ettiquette. The wide variety of ingredients, forms, and cultural customs associated with the cuisine may be foreign to many people, and even those with previous knowledge might be misinformed about the specifics of traditional etiquette. Provided with the right preparations and information, one may find the world of sushi to be quite rewarding.

How to Eat Sushi

The following sites provide information, instruction, and/or guidelines for eating various forms sushi. Sushi is generally eaten with hands or chopsticks, depending on the type of sushi, and the preference of the consumer.

Top Pages for Eating Instruction

Below are several sites with various content to show acceptable methods of eating sushi.

Sushi Eating HOWTO | General Instruction

A comprehensive site detailing everything from how to choose a sushi restaurant, to signs that you aren't welcome in Japanese sushi bars. The site is targeted at Westerners, and covers everything one could possibly want to know about eating sushi in traditional Japanese settings. Though the site is text-heavy, content is straight-forward and easy to understand, and many example images are included.

Sushi Encyclopedism | General Instruction

A great page with information for both beginners, experts, and perfectionists. Teaches the basics of soy sauce usage and using chopsticks or hands to eat different types of sushi (nigiri, gunkan, etc). Also includeds suggestions and things that should avoided when eating sushi, as well as how to order at a sushi bar.

Chef Taro | General Instruction

A great lesson in comic-form going over everything you need to know about eating sushi. Whether to use your hands or your chopsticks, how much of which condiment to use, and even illustrations on the best way to use those condiments -- this comic is a great way to learn both beginner and advanced sushi tips. There is also a short description of Japanese terms used following the comic.

Trevor Corson | General Instruction

Acclaimed author Trevor Corson has written books and articles about topics including food, health, religion, and foreign affairs. In this article, Corson writes about sushi bar culture and discusses common misconceptiosn about sushi-eating. The article also teaches readers how to order and eat sushi in a sushi bar setting.

InfoBarrel | Instruction - Chopsticks for Sushi

An article explaining how and when to use chopsticks with sushi. As well as actual instruction on the mechanics of using chopsticks, the article also goes over where to place chopsticks at different points of a meal, and also the proper way to grab food from a communal dish.

wikiHow | Instruction - Temaki (Hand Rolls)

Temaki, unlike other types of sushi, are prepared by the consumer and eaten shortly after being prepared. This site contains step-by-step instruction, with pictures, on how to roll and eat temaki (hand rolls).
