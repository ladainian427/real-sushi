Health

Sushi is often considered a healthy, protein-rich option by many. Though this can be true, there are many things that can compromise this, depending on the types of ingredients used. Many health-concious consumers also worry about the dangers of parasites and mercury-poisoning in raw fish. There are many guidelines that, when followed, can help to make sushi a safe and healthy cuisine.

Safety of Sushi

Consumers of sushi must always be aware that there are risks involved in eating any raw food, including the raw fish used in sushi. Those with immune system complications, or pregnant women, are often advised not to ingest raw foods. Another huge concern is high levels of mercury in tuna and several other types of fish. The newest risk is radiation resulting from the Tohoku area earthquake and tsunami in 2011. With the right resources, consumers can be informed and stay safe from unnecessary risks.
