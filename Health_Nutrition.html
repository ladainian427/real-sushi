Health

Sushi is often considered a healthy, protein-rich option by many. Though this can be true, there are many things that can compromise this, depending on the types of ingredients used. Many health-concious consumers also worry about the dangers of parasites and mercury-poisoning in raw fish. There are many guidelines that, when followed, can help to make sushi a safe and healthy cuisine.

Nutrition in Sushi

Although sushi can be part of a healthy, balanced diet, they are many considerations that must be taken regarding the type of ingredients in the sushi. It is incorrect to simply label sushi as "healthy," as it is important to understand the different nutritional values of the individual ingredients going into each type of sushi.

Top Pages for Sushi Nutritional Information

Below are some sites with information regarding nutritional values in sushi and sushi-related ingredients.

Sushi Encyclopedism | Comprehensive

Very good site with lots of data regarding the calories and nutritional values of many types of fish and ingredients used in sushi. The site also include data on the estimated values of common Western sushi rolls based on their ingredients from various sources. Uses images to allow users to put words to actual sushi.

Sushi FAQ | Comprehensive

Another great site with tons of data. Calories, carbs, and even Weight Watchers points are among catergies in a table full of data, organized by types of sushi along the top (rolls, nigiri, sashimi, etc.). The table even includes information for other things commonly found with sushi, such as edamame or condiments.

Sushi Encyclopedia | General Information

A basic site that touches bases with the common topics regarding sushi and nutrition. Has a section with calories of some fish and rolls, as well as an article on health benefits and dieting.

Is Sushi Healthy? | General Information

This site starts off by presenting users with several relatable scenarios, explaining how sushi may or may not be healthy depending on the situation. The content goes over calories and fat in sushi and it's relation to dieting. Overall good site, although the data doesn't go into depth like the sites linked above.

wiseGEEK | Article

The article titled, "Is Sushi Good for You?" gives a general overview of the pros and cons to sushi and how certain healthy aspects can be compromised due to certain ingredients. Short and easy to understand, although there are no relevent images to illustrate the topics.
